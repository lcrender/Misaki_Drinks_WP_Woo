3. Personal data we collect

Depending on how you interact with our website, we may collect the following categories of personal data:

  • Identity and contact data: name, billing and delivery address, email address, telephone number, and document type/number where required for order processing or legal compliance.
  • Account data: username, password (stored in encrypted form), order history, and account preferences if you register an account.
  • Transaction data: details of products purchased, order values, payment status, delivery information, invoices, and communications relating to your order.
  • Payment data: payment method details. Full payment card details are processed by our payment service providers and are not stored by us.
  • Technical and usage data: IP address, browser type, device information, pages visited, and cookies or similar technologies.
  • Communication data: messages you send us through contact forms, email, or customer support channels.

4. How we use your data and legal bases

We use your personal data only where permitted by law. The main legal bases we rely on are:

  • Performance of a contract: to process and deliver your orders, manage your account, and provide customer support.
  • Legal obligation: to comply with tax, accounting, anti-fraud, product traceability, and other legal requirements applicable to the sale of alcoholic beverages in the EU.
  • Legitimate interests: to operate and improve our website, prevent misuse, ensure security, and handle complaints, provided these interests are not overridden by your rights.
  • Consent: where required for marketing communications or non-essential cookies. You may withdraw consent at any time.

5. Orders, payments, and delivery

When you place an order, we use your personal data to process payment, verify eligibility to purchase alcoholic products, arrange delivery, issue invoices, and communicate with you about your order. We may also use your data to prevent fraudulent transactions and to handle returns or complaints.

Payment processing is carried out by secure third-party payment providers. We recommend that you review their privacy policies for information on how they handle payment data.

6. Cookies and similar technologies

Our website uses cookies and similar technologies to ensure proper functionality, remember preferences where applicable, and operate essential features of the online store (such as session, cart, and checkout). Some cookies are strictly necessary for the operation of the website and do not require consent under applicable EU law.

We currently do not use non-essential analytics or marketing cookies. If we introduce such technologies in the future, we will ask for your consent through our cookie banner before they are activated, and you will be able to withdraw or change your preferences at any time.

You can manage your cookie preferences at any time using the Manage cookies link in the website footer, or by visiting our Cookie Policy. You may also control cookies through your browser settings. Please note that disabling certain necessary cookies may affect the functionality of the website or checkout process.

For more detail about the cookies used on this website, please read our Cookie Policy.

7. Sharing your data

We do not sell your personal data. We may share your data with trusted third parties only where necessary, including:

  • payment service providers;
  • delivery and logistics partners;
  • IT, hosting, and website service providers;
  • professional advisers such as accountants or legal advisers;
  • public authorities where required by law.

All service providers are required to process personal data securely and only for specified purposes.

8. International transfers

Your personal data is primarily processed within the European Economic Area (EEA). If we transfer data outside the EEA, we will ensure appropriate safeguards are in place, such as Standard Contractual Clauses or an adequacy decision by the European Commission, in accordance with GDPR requirements.

9. Data retention

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary for the purposes described in this policy, including to satisfy legal, accounting, or reporting requirements. Order and invoice data is generally retained for the period required by applicable tax and commercial law. Marketing data is retained until you unsubscribe or withdraw consent.

10. Your rights

Under the GDPR, you have the right to:

  • request access to your personal data;
  • request correction of inaccurate data;
  • request erasure of your data in certain circumstances;
  • request restriction of processing;
  • object to processing based on legitimate interests;
  • request data portability, where applicable;
  • withdraw consent at any time, where processing is based on consent;
  • lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority.

If you are located in the Netherlands, you may contact the Dutch Data Protection Authority (Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens). If you are located in another EU Member State, you may contact your local supervisory authority.

11. Marketing communications

Where permitted by law, we may send you information about our products, promotions, or events. You can opt out of marketing communications at any time by using the unsubscribe link in our emails or by contacting us directly.

12. Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorised access, loss, misuse, or alteration. However, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is completely secure.

13. Children

Our website and products are intended only for adults who meet the legal drinking age in their country. We do not knowingly collect personal data from minors. If you believe we have collected data from a minor, please contact us so we can delete it promptly.

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, legal requirements, or website functionality. The updated version will be published on this page with a revised “Last updated” date.
